Designing a User-Friendly Ski Film System

This project was my dissertation project for MSc Computer Science at St Andrews 21/22. The project was awarded 100% and won the medal for the best dissertation on the course.

Download PDF

Abstract

Humans have recorded events since 37,000 BP. In modern times, monumental and non-monumental events are constantly recorded through videos and pictures. A child’s first steps, a graduation, or the completion of a marathon are as easily recordable as a selfie on the sofa or a picture of this week’s grocery list. The skiing environment presents an area where memorable events remain difficult to capture. Handheld cameras such as the GoPro or smartphone cameras have made this recording easier, but as they are not designed specifically for the skiing environment, they still fall short of being “user-friendly”. This project aims to tackle the challenges of designing a system capable of recording memorable events in a skiing environment in a user-friendly manner. The project follows a rigorous design process as well as usability literature, eventually producing a working prototype in the form of a series of cameras capturing footage directed by a location tracking mobile application. The success of the design is then evaluated in a subsequent usability study which assesses its success as a user-friendly system.

Introduction

A skiing holiday is no exception to the fact that when people are creating memorable moments, they like to capture those moments to reflect on and share. However, unlike other memorable moments such as a child’s first nativity, going to a football game, or visiting Disney Land, the landscape, environment, and nature of skiing makes capturing those moments incredibly difficult. Skiing is a fast and dangerous sport, carried out in extreme and treacherous conditions. In one sense this adds to the memorability of the events which take place, these out of the ordinary variables increase the worthiness of those memories being captured. However simultaneously, these variables make those memories more difficult to capture and make for a frustrating experience of using the existing solutions for capturing ski film.

This project focuses on designing and building a user-friendly ski film system by following a user-centered design process. The motivation for the project is that the current solutions for capturing skiing footage provide a poor user experience which results in memorable moments, which the user would like to capture, going uncaptured and being lost.
